Your 5-Minute Start Guide

Want to start using AI today? Here's what you can do right now, what needs approval, and what to avoid.

Free Options Available Now

These tools are available to NHS staff today with no additional approval needed for administrative use.

  • Copilot Chat (free for NHS.net users)
  • NotebookLM (free, Google account)
  • Perplexity (free tier available)
  • Claude/ChatGPT (free tiers, no patient data)

What You Can Do This Week

Practical tasks you can accomplish with freely available AI tools.

  • Summarise clinical guidelines
  • Draft business case templates
  • Create teaching presentations
  • Generate audit proformas
  • Research evidence for QIPs

What Needs Trust Approval

These require DPIA, Caldicott Guardian sign-off, and local governance.

  • AI clinical documentation (ambient scribes)
  • Diagnostic decision support tools
  • Any tool processing patient data
  • Integrated workflow automation

Red Lines: Never Do This

These actions could breach IG, invalidate indemnity, or cause patient harm.

  • Never enter patient data into public AI
  • Never use AI for diagnosis without oversight
  • Never assume anonymisation is sufficient
  • Never bypass Trust governance for clinical tools

Remember

Start with administrative tasks where you can verify accuracy and no patient data is involved. Build confidence and demonstrate value before advocating for clinical AI tools.

Data Protection: Where CAN You Safely Use AI?

It's not all restrictions. Here's where patient data CAN be used safely with AI ‚Äî and the critical boundaries.

‚úì SAFE TO USE

NHS M365 Copilot

National DPIA completed (Sept 2025). Enterprise Data Protection enabled. Data encrypted in transit and at rest. User data NOT used to retrain models.

  • ‚úì Meeting transcription & summaries
  • ‚úì Email drafting assistance
  • ‚úì Excel analysis (no patient data)
  • ‚úó Patient data in prompts still prohibited
‚úì SAFE TO USE

Dragon Copilot (Clinical)

MHRA Class I medical device. Meets NHS DTAC, DCB-0129, ISO 27001. Used in 7 NHS organisations during private preview with 10,000+ consultations.

  • ‚úì Ambient clinical documentation
  • ‚úì EPR integration
  • ‚úì Patient consent process built-in
  • ‚ö† Requires Trust procurement & DPIA
‚úì SAFE TO USE

Trust-Approved Clinical AI

Systems with completed local DPIA, Caldicott Guardian approval, and appropriate data processing agreements (e.g., Heidi at Walsall).

  • ‚úì Approved by your Trust governance
  • ‚úì Local DPIA completed
  • ‚úì Data processing agreement in place
  • ‚úì Staff training provided

üö´ NEVER Safe: Public AI Systems with Patient Data

The following should NEVER be used with any patient-identifiable or sensitive information ‚Äî even data you believe is "anonymised":

ChatGPT (consumer version)
Claude (consumer version)
Gemini (consumer version)
Any unapproved AI tool

Why? Simply removing names does not create anonymisation. Combination of rare conditions, ages, and locations can identify patients. MDU/MPS may not provide indemnity for data breaches involving unapproved AI systems.

The Key Principle

If a system has completed a DPIA, has Trust governance approval, and you've received training on its appropriate use ‚Äî it's likely safe for its intended purpose. If in doubt, ask your Caldicott Guardian or IG team before use.
