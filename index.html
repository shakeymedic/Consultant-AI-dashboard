AI Limitations You Must Know

AI is powerful, but it fails in specific, predictable ways. Evidence-based risks.

üé≠ Hallucination (8-20%)

Studies estimate hallucination rates of 8-20% in clinical AI. LLM-generated notes showed 9.6% error rate vs 2.0% for physician notes. Documented cases include fabricated clinical summaries and fake PubMed IDs.

üìÖ Knowledge Cutoff

ChatGPT 5.2: Aug 2025. Gemini 3: Jan 2025. Claude 4.5 Opus: Mar-Aug 2025. LLMs may not know latest guidelines, new drugs, or recent recalls. Always cross-reference with current NICE guidance.

üòä Sycophancy Bias

npj Digital Medicine (Oct 2025): GPT models complied with incorrect instructions 100% of the time when users made false claims. Models "prioritise helpfulness over critical thinking" ‚Äî dangerous in healthcare.

üìä Training Data Bias

Documented cases: Optum algorithm prioritised healthier white patients. Pulse oximetry overestimates saturation in non-white patients (3√ó more likely undetected hypoxemia in Black patients).

How to Get AI Approved

The pathway from "I want to use AI" to "We're live".

1

Check DTAC Compliance First

NHS DTAC portal shows pre-assessed products. Mandatory for all digital health technologies entering NHS. Covers clinical safety, data protection, security, interoperability.

2

Contact Digital Clinical Safety

DCB0129 applies to manufacturers; DCB0160 applies to NHS organisations deploying AI. Contact: england.digitalsafety@nhs.net. 2025 review underway for AI-specific challenges.

3

Engage Caldicott Guardian

8 Caldicott Principles (expanded Dec 2020). Principle 8: Inform patients about how their data is used‚Äîincluding by AI. National Data Guardian: gov.uk/national-data-guardian

4

Follow HM Treasury Business Case Model

NHS business cases follow Green Book Five Case Model. AI can help draft but never input confidential data. HFMA AI Guidance available for NHS finance context.

‚ö†Ô∏è Different Trusts have different risk appetites. MHRA regulates AI as medical devices under UK MDR 2002. Most current AI is Class I (self-certification); new rules will upclassify most to Class IIa minimum by June 2028/2030.

AI Limitations Every Clinician Must Know

AI fails in specific, predictable ways. Understanding these helps you use AI safely.

üé≠

Hallucination

Studies estimate hallucination rates of 8-20% in clinical AI. LLM-generated notes showed 9.6% error rate versus 2.0% for physician notes. Documented cases include fabricated summaries and fake PubMed IDs.

Knowledge Cutoff

Current cutoffs: ChatGPT 5.2 (Aug 2025), Gemini 3 (Jan 2025), Claude 4.5 Opus (Mar-Aug 2025). LLMs may not know latest guidelines or recalled medications. Always cross-reference with NICE.

Models have training cutoffs

Sycophancy Bias

npj Digital Medicine (Oct 2025): GPT models complied with incorrect instructions 100% of the time when users made false claims. "Prioritise helpfulness over critical thinking" ‚Äî dangerous in healthcare.

100% compliance with false claims Source: Nature Digital Medicine ‚Üí

Training Data Bias

Documented: Optum algorithm prioritised healthier white patients. Pulse oximetry overestimates saturation in non-white patients (3√ó more likely undetected hypoxemia in Black patients).

3√ó undetected hypoxemia risk

DCB Compliance Gap

75% lack DCB compliance Source: JMIR 2025 ‚Üí

Context Limits

AI can "forget" information from earlier in long conversations. It may contradict itself or lose track of important clinical details you provided at the start.

Long conversations risky

The Golden Rule

Treat AI output like a confident junior doctor's suggestion: often helpful, sometimes wrong, always requires verification. MDU and MPS confirm: you remain liable for AI-assisted decisions.

Step-by-Step: FEG from Teams Meeting to Kaizen

Part 1: Record & Summarise (NHS Copilot)

  1. Schedule your FEG meeting in Teams with your trainee
  2. Start the meeting and enable transcription/recording
  3. Have your educational supervision discussion as normal
  4. After the meeting, Copilot generates a summary with:
    • Key discussion points
    • Learning objectives covered
    • Agreed actions and PDP items
    • Follow-up dates
  5. Copy the summary or export as text

Part 2: Form Filling (Comet/Agentic Browser)

  1. Open Comet browser and paste your Copilot summary
  2. Give the instruction: "Log into Kaizen at [URL], navigate to [trainee's name], open a new FEG form, and populate the fields using this meeting summary"
  3. Watch as Comet:
    • Logs in with your credentials
    • Navigates to the correct trainee
    • Opens the FEG form
    • Maps summary content to form fields
  4. Review each field the agent has populated
  5. Make any manual edits, then submit

Time saved: What typically takes 30-45 minutes of typing and form navigation can be reduced to 5-10 minutes of review and submission.
