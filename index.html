What IS AI, Actually?

A 2-minute primer for the busy consultant.

üß† Large Language Models

AI trained on billions of documents. It predicts the most likely next word based on patterns. Think of it as autocomplete on steroids ‚Äî exceptionally good at writing, summarising, and analysing text.

Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama

üí¨ Chatbots & Interfaces

ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot ‚Äî these are user-friendly interfaces to interact with LLMs. You type a question (called a "prompt"), and they generate a response. Quality depends on how you ask.

Better prompts = better results

ü§ñ Agentic AI (New!)

The next evolution: AI that can take actions on your behalf ‚Äî clicking buttons, filling forms, navigating websites. Still emerging but powerful for admin tasks.

Examples: Comet, Operator, Computer Use

‚ö†Ô∏è The Critical Insight

AI doesn't "know" things like you do. It generates statistically plausible text. This means it can be confidently wrong ("hallucination"). It will invent citations and fabricate statistics. Always verify important facts.

Data Protection & Governance

Complete guidance on what's safe, what needs approval, and what's never allowed.

The Traffic Light Framework

‚úì

Green Zone ‚Äî Use Now

No patient data involved. No approval needed.

Examples
  • Drafting SOPs, guidelines, and policies
  • Writing business cases and proposals
  • Creating teaching materials and presentations
  • Meeting summaries (NHS Copilot)
  • Analysing fully anonymised aggregate audit data
  • Job descriptions and interview questions
  • General clinical topic research

Tools: NHS Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, NotebookLM ‚Äî any tool, as long as no patient data.

‚ö†

Amber Zone ‚Äî Trust Approval Required

Involves patient data or clinical workflows. Requires formal approval.

Examples
  • AI clinical documentation (ambient scribes)
  • Diagnostic support tools (fracture AI, imaging)
  • AI-assisted triage or decision support
  • Any tool processing patient information
  • Workflow automation touching clinical systems
Requirements
  • Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
  • Caldicott Guardian sign-off
  • DTAC compliance verification
  • DCB0129/0160 clinical safety standards
  • Staff training and competency assessment
‚úó

Red Zone ‚Äî Never Allowed

ANY patient-identifiable data in public AI. No exceptions.

Never Do This
  • Pasting clinic letters or discharge summaries into ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini
  • Uploading patient notes or scanned documents
  • Asking AI about specific patient cases
  • Sharing "anonymised" data that could be re-identified
  • Taking photos of patient notes and uploading
  • Copy-pasting from EPR systems into AI
Consequences

GDPR breach, GMC investigation, loss of indemnity, Trust disciplinary action, potential patient harm.

The 8 Caldicott Principles

These principles govern all use of patient-identifiable information, including AI.

1

Justify the purpose for using confidential information

2

Don't use personal confidential data unless absolutely necessary

3

Use minimum necessary personal confidential data

4

Access on need-to-know basis only

5

Everyone must understand their responsibilities

6

Comply with the law

7

Duty to share for care is as important as protecting confidentiality

8

Inform patients about how their data is used ‚Äî including AI (added 2020)

How to Get AI Approved at Your Trust

If you want to implement an AI tool that processes patient data (Amber Zone), here's the pathway.

1

Check DTAC Compliance First

Check the NHS DTAC Portal to see if the tool is already assessed. If not listed, vendor needs to complete assessment (can take months).

2

Contact Digital/IT Team

Your Trust has a process for technology requests. Contact CCIO or complete a Technology Request form.

3

Engage Clinical Safety Team

Your Clinical Safety Officer will assess risks. DCB0129 applies to manufacturers; DCB0160 applies to NHS deployment.

4

Caldicott Guardian Review

For any tool processing patient data, your Caldicott Guardian must review and approve against the 8 Principles.

5

Complete DPIA

Data Protection Impact Assessment documents risks and mitigations. Your IG team will have a template.

6

Procurement & Implementation

Procurement, contract review, technical integration, staff training, go-live with monitoring plan.

Typical Timeline: Expect 3-12 months from request to go-live, depending on complexity and existing approvals.

AI Limitations You Must Know

AI fails in specific, predictable ways. Understanding these keeps you safe.

üé≠

Hallucination

8-20% Rate

AI confidently states false information ‚Äî fabricated citations, invented statistics, fictional guidelines. Studies show 8-20% hallucination rates even in medical contexts.

‚Üí Always verify facts, citations, and statistics from primary sources
üòä

Sycophancy Bias

High Risk

AI tends to agree with you, even when you're wrong. Research shows 100% compliance with incorrect instructions when users made false claims.

‚Üí Challenge outputs ‚Äî Ask AI to critique itself
üìÖ

Knowledge Cutoff

Medium Risk

AI training has fixed cutoff dates. It may not know recent guidelines, drug recalls, or new evidence. ChatGPT/Claude: ~Aug 2025.

‚Üí Check currency with NICE/BNF directly
üìä

Training Data Bias

Medium Risk

AI reflects biases in training data. Documented cases of algorithms disadvantaging certain demographics. Pulse oximetry AI overestimates in non-white patients.

‚Üí Apply clinical judgment, especially for diverse populations

üèÜ The Golden Rule

Treat AI output like a confident junior doctor's suggestion:

  • Often helpful and saves you time
  • Sometimes wrong in ways that aren't immediately obvious
  • Always requires verification and supervision
  • You remain accountable for the final decision
